Kingda Ka: The Tallest Coaster

History

Kingda Ka is a roller coaster located at Jackson, New Jersey's Six Flags Great Adventure, conveniently found between New York City and Philadelphia. This ride opened in 2005 at a cost of $25 million USD. Built by Intamin Amusement Rides, Kingda Ka uses a hydraulic launch to accelerate riders up to its record-breaking heights and speeds. The attraction accends a giant vertical tower, with a 90 degree left-hand twist on the way up, and a 270 degree right-hand spiral on its way down. The coaster quickly finishes with a large speed hill that doubles as a brake section.

Statistics

  • Maximum Height: 456ft/139m
  • Maximum Drop: 418ft/127.4m
  • Length: 3,118ft/950.4m
  • Speed: 128mph/206kph in 3.5 seconds
  • Maximum Vertical Angle: 90 degrees
  • Duration: 28 seconds

Records & Reception

This attraction opened with three world records. Kingda Ka took the world height, drop, and speed records from another Intamin hydraulic launch coaster that opened two years prior: Top Thrill Dragster at Sandusky, Ohio's Cedar Point. The record-breaking coaster actually sparked controversy amongst roller coaster enthusiats when it was announced in 2004, as the ride's layout is extremely similar to its Ohioan predecessor, just with slighly boosted stats and changing Top Thrill's flat braking runout to a 129ft tall speed/braking hill. Contoversial or not, Kingda Ka remains an impressive feat of engineering, and though it has since lost its speed record, it doesn't look like its impressive height record will be beat anytime soon.

World Records

  • Tallest Height on a Roller Coaster at 456ft: 2005-Present
  • Tallest Drop on a Roller Coaster at 418ft: 2005-Present
  • Fastest Roller Coaster at 128mph: 2005-2010
